Everything, on your machine

An AI partner that knows what you've written

Write in a fast, private markdown editor. Then let an AI partner search your own notes, work from documents you bring in, and carry a piece all the way to wherever you publish. Same app — your call, every time.

✎

Markdown editor

CodeMirror 6 with a formatting toolbar, syntax highlighting, and a live GitHub-flavored preview rendered by the same engine that drives every export.

✦

AI writing partner

Streaming chat with your document as context. Multiple threads per doc, token usage shown, and a Voice & tone you set once so everything sounds like you.

⌕

Search your notes

The assistant semantically searches everything you've written and answers grounded in your own documents. A small embedding model runs entirely on your machine — nothing is sent out. Opt-in: download a model once in Settings.

⤓

Bring in your documents

Import Markdown, plain text, PDF, and Word .docx — either as editable documents, or as read-only Sources the AI can search but you never touch. Add and remove them freely.

⌁

Inline edit

Select a sentence, get a streamed rewrite previewed right in place, then accept or reject — no round trip through a chat box.

◍

Web search, when you want it

Toggle web search on in a chat and the assistant can pull in fresh information from the web. Off by default — it only reaches out when you ask it to.

⧉

Write once, multiply

Write the piece once, then turn it into platform-native variants — blog, newsletter, LinkedIn, X thread — each a linked, editable document. No rewriting it five times.

↗

Export anywhere

Real Word .docx with heading styles, numbering, checkboxes, hyperlinks and tables. Plus X threads and X Articles, LinkedIn-ready text, and clean self-contained HTML.

▢

Yours, locally

Ideas inbox, version history, and folders keep everything in one SQLite database on your machine. Keys live in the macOS Keychain — the only thing that leaves is what you send your AI provider.

How it flows

Whatever you're writing — a journal entry, a blog post, a paper, a skill doc — start it once and take it all the way to wherever it needs to go.

1

Capture & gather

Drop a half-formed thought into the Ideas inbox, start a fresh markdown doc, or bring in existing notes, PDFs, and Word files.

2

Draft with AI

The partner works from your document, your own notes, and any Sources you've added — writing in the voice you defined once.

3

Reshape & share

Multiply one piece into a blog post, a thread, or a doc, keep every version in history, then export clean to Word, HTML, or wherever you publish.

Your studio, your machine

Plume is local-first and bring-your-own-key. Your writing, your notes, and the model that searches them all stay on your machine. No subscription, no lock-in — just a tool that gets out of your way.
